EXECUTIVE OVERVIEW
================================================================================

QuickBite Express, a Bengaluru-based food delivery platform founded in 2020,
experienced a severe operational and reputational crisis in June 2025. This 
analysis examines the crisis impact, identifies root causes, and presents a 
comprehensive recovery strategy with quantified financial projections.

KEY FINDINGS AT A GLANCE:

📉 ORDER VOLUME: Dropped 59% during crisis period (June 2025)
   • Pre-Crisis: 22,761 orders/month
   • Crisis: 9,293 orders/month  
   • Current: 8,689 orders/month (38% of baseline)

👥 CUSTOMER BASE: Lost 72.5% of active customer base
   • Lost Customers: 73,000 (69%)
   • At-Risk Customers: 3,453 (3%)
   • Recovered Customers: 10,603 (10%)
   • New Customers: 13,807 (13%)

📦 OPERATIONAL COLLAPSE: Critical service degradation
   • On-Time Delivery: 92% → 41% (51 percentage point drop)
   • Average Delivery Time: 39.5 → 60.1 minutes (+20.6 min)
   • Cancellation Rate: 6.1% → 12.1% (doubled)

⭐ SATISFACTION CRISIS: Trust destroyed
   • Average Rating: 4.50 → 2.47 stars (2.03 star drop)
   • Sentiment Score: +0.752 → -0.266 (shifted negative)
   • Negative Reviews: 0% → 25.1%

💰 FINANCIAL IMPACT: ₹19.15M cumulative loss
   • Monthly Revenue: ₹7.52M → ₹2.68M (64% decline)
   • Cumulative Loss (4 months): ₹19.15M
   • Projected Annual Impact: ₹58M+ if unaddressed

🏪 PARTNER CHURN: 27% restaurant partner loss
   • Pre-Crisis Partners: 19,919
   • Current Partners: 14,581
   • Churned: 5,338 restaurants

================================================================================
CRISIS TIMELINE & ROOT CAUSES
================================================================================

JUNE 2025 CRISIS SEQUENCE:

Week 1 (June 1-7): Initial Incident
  • Viral social media post regarding food safety violations at partner 
    restaurant
  • #QuickBiteDisaster trends with 2M+ views
  • Immediate 15% order drop

Week 2 (June 8-14): Compounding Factors
  • Monsoon season causes delivery infrastructure failures
  • Week-long service disruptions in multiple cities
  • Cumulative order drop reaches 40%

Week 3 (June 15-21): Competitive Pressure
  • Competitors launch "Safe & Fast" campaigns
  • Aggressive customer poaching with incentives
  • Mass customer exodus accelerates

Week 4 (June 22-30): Stabilization at Crisis Level
  • Orders stabilize at -60% from baseline
  • Partner restaurants begin platform migration
  • Recovery efforts initiated but ineffective

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:

1. SYSTEMIC OPERATIONAL FAILURE
   • Inadequate delivery partner capacity during peak demand
   • No weather-adaptive routing or capacity planning
   • Delayed response to infrastructure issues
   • Result: On-time delivery collapsed to 41%

2. QUALITY ASSURANCE GAPS
   • Insufficient restaurant partner vetting
   • No real-time hygiene monitoring
   • Reactive vs. proactive quality management
   • Result: Food safety incident enabled

3. CRISIS COMMUNICATION BREAKDOWN
   • Slow response to social media crisis (>24 hours)
   • Generic, non-personalized customer communications
   • Overwhelmed customer service (>2 hour response times)
   • Result: Trust erosion and viral negativity

4. COMPETITIVE VULNERABILITY
   • No customer loyalty program or retention mechanisms
   • Price-based differentiation easily matched
   • No unique value proposition beyond selection
   • Result: Easy customer migration to competitors

RISK ASSESSMENT & MITIGATION
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

RISK 1: Lower Than Expected Customer Recovery Rate
Likelihood: Medium | Impact: High
Mitigation: Phased incentive increases, A/B test messaging, target high-value
            customers first, extend campaign duration if needed
Contingency: ₹2.21M reserve fund for additional incentives

RISK 2: Competitive Response (Matching Our Offers)
Likelihood: High | Impact: Medium
Mitigation: Speed of execution (first-mover advantage), focus on operational
            excellence (hard to copy), build loyalty mechanisms beyond price
Contingency: Prepared to invest additional ₹5M in retention if needed

RISK 3: Technology Implementation Delays
Likelihood: Medium | Impact: Medium
Mitigation: Phased rollout, pilot programs, backup manual processes,
            vendor SLAs with penalties
Contingency: Manual workarounds, hire additional ops staff temporarily

RISK 4: Restaurant Partner Rejection
Likelihood: Low | Impact: High
Mitigation: Attractive commission reductions, guaranteed order volumes,
            marketing support, fast payouts
Contingency: Accelerate new partner acquisition, temporary delivery-only model

RISK 5: Insufficient Budget Approval
Likelihood: Low | Impact: Critical
Mitigation: Phased funding requests, demonstrate early wins before full
            deployment, secure partial approval
Contingency: Prioritize Pillar 1 & 2, delay Pillar 3 if necessary
